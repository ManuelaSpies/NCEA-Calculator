Copyright is the right that are given to the creator and/or owner of an original content upon creation. It gives right of ownership to things you created, such as books, or programs which applies to this case. Copyright by default is very “closed”, i.e. the creator has many rights and nobody else has any rights, and if anyone owns, sells, modifies, or uses it can be punished through the law. However, the creator of the property can grant people license that allow them to use the property under given restrictions, i.e. time period, whenever they can edit it, whenever they can sell it or publish it, and costs for licenses. There are various types of pre-written licenses, and individual ones, under which these things are made available.

This is relevant to me. Thus, through licenses, I can use various written codes and similar things in my project. I am using bootstrap (licensed under the MIT License), Google Charts (under Apache license) and other things that don’t need to be noted going by Mr Harford. The outcome only used open licenses, meaning licenses for which you do not need to pay and where the restrictions aren’t strict. The licenses allow me to use the code on my project, due to the circumstances and limits/lack of limits.

Sqlite is public domain and thus usable, as nobody owns the copyright. Because of copyright implications, I used my own photos and obtained verbal consent of the models.
